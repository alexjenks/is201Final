Background

The Wasatch Range of Utah is home to some of the best ski resorts, famed by its steep terrain, as well as snow depth and quality. Sitting in a very dry region of the United States, the snow that falls in the Wasatch is very dry. With a water content of around 8%, it is some of the lightest snow around. That combined with the large amount of snow that falls, Alta for example receives about 500 inches of snow annually, makes for an amazing powder experince.

Safety

Now that you know you want to ski Utah, there are some safety precautions that all should be aware of:

  1. When driving up the canyons, always adhere to the traction law. It is there for the safety and convenience of all skiers.
  2. If you are backcountry skiing, always have the necessary safety gear:
      A probe. A Shovel An Avalanche Beacon Most Importantly, A BUDDY!
  3. Make sure your skis, bindings, and other pieces gear are working properly.
  4. Bring a first aid kit.
  5. Pack water and snacks.
